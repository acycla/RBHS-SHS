Strands News

The SMAW (Shielded Metal Arc Welding) strand is dedicated to equipping students with practical skills and knowledge in welding technology. Students in this strand learn the art and science of metal joining using shielded metal arc welding techniques.

SMAW students gain hands-on experience in welding processes, safety protocols, and metal fabrication. Emphasis is placed on precision, craftsmanship, and adherence to industry standards in welding practices.

The curriculum covers various aspects of welding, including interpreting welding symbols, understanding metallurgy, and mastering different welding positions. SMAW students develop proficiency in using welding equipment, selecting appropriate welding materials, and ensuring the quality of welded joints.

Career Opportunities

Graduates of the SMAW strand can pursue rewarding career opportunities in the field of welding and metal fabrication, including:

  • Welder
  • Metal Fabricator
  • Welding Inspector
  • Welding Technician
  • Structural Steel Worker
  • Pipefitter
