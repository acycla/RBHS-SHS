Strands News

The STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) strand is an interdisciplinary field that integrates these four key areas to solve complex real-world problems. Students in STEM programs gain a deep understanding of scientific principles, technological innovations, engineering processes, and mathematical concepts.

STEM education emphasizes hands-on learning and critical thinking skills. Students engage in projects and activities that encourage creativity, collaboration, and problem-solving. This strand prepares individuals for a wide range of careers, from research and development to technological innovation and engineering design.

With a focus on practical applications, STEM graduates are well-equipped to address the challenges of the modern world. Whether it's developing new technologies, designing sustainable solutions, or advancing scientific knowledge, individuals with a background in STEM contribute significantly to the progress of society.

STEM education opens doors to diverse career opportunities, including:

  • Research Scientist
  • Doctor
  • Mechanical Engineer
  • Astrophysicist
  • Biomedical Engineer
  • Biologist
  • Environmental Scientist
  • Marine Engineer
  • Architect
